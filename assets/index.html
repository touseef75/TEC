Pharynx & Salivary glands

Pharynx :
It is the region between the mouth and the esophagus.Pharynx leads to a narrow tubular canal known as the oesophagus. Oesophagus opens into a structure known as a crop which is a sac-like structure used to store the food.
Salivary glands :
The cockroach salivary gland consists of secretory acini with peripheral ion-transporting cells and central protein-producing cells, an extensive duct system, and a pair of reservoirs. Salivation is controlled by serotonergic and dopaminergic innervation.

Crop & Gizzard

Crop :
Crop is the dilated distal part of oesophagus acting as food reservoir.
Salivary glands :
The gizzard is a kind of muscular stomach in cockroaches. It includes sharp teeth which are able to grind the food into small parts. The alimentary canal is a tubular arrangement, expanding from the mouth to the anus found in the body nook of cockroach.

Mesenteron & IIeum

Mesenteron :
It is a short, tubular line with glandular endoderm.It is a membrane growth present In the stomach of a cockroach lies between the gizzard and midgut. - Its function is to prevent the entry of the food from the midgut into the gizzard.
IIeum :
The ileum absorbs bile acids, fluid, and vitamin B-12. Finger-shaped structures called villi line the entire small intestine.

Colon & Rectum

Colon : In cockroaches, the most important site for symbiotic digestion is the colon.The colon removes water, salt, and some nutrients forming stool. It is a highly coiled structure. Muscles line the colon's walls, squeezing its contents along. Billions of bacteria coat the colon and its contents, living in a healthy balance with the body.

Rectum :
Absorption of water and ions occur through rectum. In cockroach lipid absorption occurs through crop. In termites and scarabaeids (White grubs) absorption occurs through ileum. In solid feeders, resorption of water from the faeces occurs in the rectum and the faeces is expelled as pellets.
